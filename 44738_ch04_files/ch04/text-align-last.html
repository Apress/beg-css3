text-align-last: right: The scientific name for the Joshua tree is yucca brevifolia. New seedlings grow about 3 inches (7.6 cm) a year for the first ten years, but then slow down to just half that rate thereafter. In spite of this slow growth, a Joshua tree can survive for hundreds of years. The tallest trees grow to a height of nearly 50 feet (15 m).
